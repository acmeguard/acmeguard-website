The strategic case for your CEO

Frame it as business risk, competitive position, and forward planning.

Business continuity risk

Risk: A single expired or compromised certificate can take a customer-facing service offline for hours.

Pattern: Outages from forgotten renewals are now an industry-wide tax.

Mitigation: AcmeGuard makes the renewal cadence a system property, not a calendar reminder.

Competitive position

The market: Most peers still treat certificates as a manual operations chore.

The opportunity: Faster, safer rollouts of new internal services without per-certificate friction.

The story: A security posture you can describe to customers, auditors, and the board.

Forward planning

Industry change: Certificate lifetimes drop from 200 days (2026) to 47 days (2029).

Implication: Manual renewal becomes operationally impossible, early adopters move first.

Position: Be the company that adapted ahead of the deadline, not the one scrambling.

Bottom line for the CEO

  • Remove certificate outages from the business-continuity risk register
  • Tell a credible certificate-governance story to customers and auditors
  • Be ready for the CA/Browser Forum lifetime cuts before competitors are
  • Free engineering time from per-cert ceremony

The security case for your CISO

Reduce attack surface, prove compliance, see everything.

Discover unauthorised certificates

The blind spot: Certificates issued without your team's knowledge, by other teams, by vendors, by attackers.

Detection: Continuous CT-log monitoring across every public CA.

Control: A central ACME gateway gates every new request through policy.

Reduce attack surface

The risk: Self-signed internals, mTLS shortcuts, and "trust me" prompts that train users to ignore warnings.

The fix: Real public-CA certificates for internal services via DNS-01, without exposing them to the internet.

Result: No more browser warnings, no more cert-based attack vectors.

Compliance evidence on demand

The requirement: SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI all need certificate lifecycle controls.

The evidence: Continuous inventory + per-event audit trail.

Efficiency: Auditors get reports straight from AcmeGuard, not from your team's calendar.

Bottom line for the CISO

  • Surface and control every certificate, including the ones you didn't issue
  • Eliminate the self-signed internal-services attack class
  • Produce compliance evidence continuously, not seasonally
  • Centralise policy without slowing teams down
